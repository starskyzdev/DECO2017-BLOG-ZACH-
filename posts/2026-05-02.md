Following our interpretation of the brief, we dove into user research. We chose the sourdough-baking community, specifically serious hobby bakers. We wanted to know what they cared about, what frustrated them, and what would make them return to an app daily. 

Our research included analysing community discussions, forum posts, and existing app store solutions to identify market gaps.

## The Core Insight: Process over Recipes

The most significant pattern we discovered is that serious bakers approach baking as both a craft and an experiment. They are not interested in simply following easy recipes; instead, they seek to understand the underlying variables involved. They monitor hydration percentages, dough temperatures, bulk fermentation times, and fold counts.

When a loaf fails or turns out exceptionally well, they want to understand the reasons. For them, tracking data is valuable only if it contributes to their learning and improvement. ## The Problem: Fragmented Tools

The biggest pain point we identified is tool fragmentation. Bakers are currently juggling multiple separate tools to manage one process:
- A calculator for hydration and baker's percentages
- A reminder app or timer for stretch-and-folds
- A notebook or spreadsheet to log the process
- Reddit or Facebook to ask for troubleshooting advice

Because these tools do not connect, comparing bakes is difficult. Furthermore, when bakers ask for advice online, they often fail to provide enough context. Posting a photo of a dense crumb without sharing the dough temperature or proofing time leads to vague, unhelpful advice.

## What Drives Daily Use?

We also explored what features would drive daily retention, a key requirement from the BlaBla brief. We found that daily use comes from habits, not inspiration alone.

The strongest habit loops for this community are:
1. **Starter maintenance:** A daily or weekly task that requires logging and reminders.
2. **Live bake support:** Time-sensitive tracking during an active bake.
3. **Structured community feedback:** Receiving actionable advice connected to their specific process data.

## The Product Opportunity: A Community-Driven Baking Lab

These insights directly shape our prototype. We are designing a "community-driven baking lab" that consolidates these fragmented tools into one hub. 

It will be a desktop-first experience that allows users to track their variables, compare outcomes, and share structured records for community critique. By ensuring every post contains the necessary context, we elevate the quality of feedback and create a genuine learning environment.
